In a paper published in 2023 by David Smith, Joseph Samuel Myers, Craig S. Kaplan, and Chaim Goodman-Strauss, the authors address a longstanding open problem concerning aperiodic monotiles, also referred to as "einsteins." The focus of the research is on topological disk tiles, and the paper provides a solution by presenting a continuous set of combinatorially equivalent aperiodic polygons. The key demonstration involves the "hat" polykite, identified as a representative example, which has the ability to form clusters known as "metatiles." Subsequently, substitution rules are defined for these metatiles, leading to tilings of the plane. The paper establishes that the hat polykite, and by extension, the entire continuum of polygons presented, exhibit aperiodic properties through a novel geometric incommensurability argument. Additionally, the authors offer a combinatorial, computer-assisted proof demonstrating that the hat polykite must inevitably form hierarchical tilings, thereby confirming its aperiodic nature. This research contributes to addressing the long-standing challenge of identifying aperiodic monotiles in the context of topological disk tiles.

Rendering the "hat" in Javascript

Motivated by the educational value and the enjoyment derived from exploring mathematical concepts through interactive programming, I find rendering the "hat" polykite in JavaScript to be a fascinating project. By creating a visual representation of the aperiodic nature of the hat, individuals can gain a deeper understanding of the underlying geometric principles and appreciate the complexity of aperiodic tilings. To achieve this in JavaScript, one might follow these steps:

  1. Define Geometric Parameters: Establish the geometric properties of the hat polykite, including vertices, edges, and angles, to accurately represent its shape.
  2. Canvas Setup: Utilize HTML5 Canvas or a similar graphics library to set up a drawing area for rendering the hat polykite on a web page.
  3. Implement Rendering Algorithm: Develop a JavaScript algorithm to calculate and draw the polygons, incorporating the defined geometric parameters.
  4. Interactive Features: Enhance the educational experience by adding interactive elements, such as zooming or dragging, to allow users to explore the rendered hat polykite dynamically.
  5. Educational Annotations: Include educational annotations or tooltips to explain key geometric concepts and the significance of the hat polykite's aperiodicity, enhancing the learning experience for users.
